Lumeva (“we”, “us”, “our”)

Last updated: 06/11/2025

This Data Protection Notice (“Notice”) explains how we collect, use, disclose and otherwise process personal data of customers in accordance with Singapore’s Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA). It also covers personal data processed on our behalf by service providers engaged by us.

Use of this Notice does not itself guarantee PDPA compliance. Ensure your internal practices align with what is stated here.

Contents

1. Definitions

Customer: an individual who contacts us about our goods/services, or who has entered into a contract with us.

Personal data: data, whether true or not, about a customer who can be identified from that data alone or with other information we are likely to have access to (e.g., name, address, email, phone, nationality, gender, date of birth, marital status, photograph).

Terms used here have the meanings given in the PDPA where the context permits.

2. Collection, Use & Disclosure

We generally collect personal data only when you (or your authorised representative) have been notified of the purposes and given consent, or where collection/use without consent is permitted or required by law.

Purposes

  • Performing obligations in connection with goods/services requested by you;
  • Responding to and processing queries, requests, applications, complaints and feedback;
  • Complying with applicable laws, regulations, codes, guidelines and assisting law enforcement and investigations;
  • Any other purposes for which you provided the information or that are incidental to the above;
  • Transmitting data to unaffiliated third parties (service providers/agents and relevant authorities, in Singapore or abroad) for these purposes.

Disclosure

We may disclose personal data where required to perform our obligations to you, and to third-party service providers and agents engaged by us for the purposes above.

These purposes may continue to apply for a reasonable period even after your relationship with us ends (e.g., to enforce contractual rights).

3. Deemed Consent by Notification

For certain secondary purposes different from the original purpose of collection, we may rely on deemed consent by notification after assessing that doing so is not likely to have an adverse effect on you and after providing an opportunity to opt out within a reasonable period.

Example: research on user behaviour.

You may later withdraw such deemed consent (see Section 5).

4. Legitimate Interests Exception

Where permitted by the PDPA, we may collect, use or disclose personal data without consent for legitimate interests of Lumeva or another person, after assessing that such interests outweigh any likely adverse effect on you.

  • Fraud detection and prevention;
  • Detection and prevention of misuse of services;
  • Network analysis to prevent fraud and financial crime, and perform credit analysis;
  • Use of data on company-issued devices to prevent data loss.

These purposes may continue for a reasonable period after the relationship ends.

5. Withdrawing Consent

Consent remains valid until withdrawn. You may withdraw consent (in whole or in part) by writing to our Data Protection Officer (see Section 11). We may need reasonable time to process your request and to explain the consequences, including any impact on our ability to provide goods/services.

We aim to process withdrawals within fourteen (14) business days. Withdrawing consent does not affect our right to continue processing where permitted or required by law.

6. Access & Correction

You may request (a) access to a copy of personal data we hold about you and information on how it has been used or disclosed, or (b) correction of your personal data. A reasonable fee may be charged for access; we will inform you of the fee before processing.

We generally respond within seven (7) business days, or otherwise notify you within thirty (30) days of when we can respond. If we are unable to provide data or make a correction, we will generally inform you of the reasons unless we are not required to do so under the PDPA.

7. Protection of Personal Data

We implement administrative, physical and technical measures to safeguard personal data, including minimised collection, access controls and password hygiene, encryption, anonymisation, patching, secure erasure of media prior to disposal, web security controls, and periodic security reviews and testing. While no method of transmission or storage is completely secure, we continuously review and enhance these measures.

8. Accuracy

We generally rely on personal data provided by you or your authorised representative. Please keep us updated if your data changes (see Section 11).

9. Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, or as required/permitted by law. We will cease retention, or anonymise data, when it is no longer needed for legal or business purposes.

10. Overseas Transfers

We generally do not transfer personal data outside Singapore. If we do, we will obtain consent where required and ensure a comparable standard of protection to the PDPA.
